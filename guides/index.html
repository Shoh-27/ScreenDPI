Common Questions

What's the difference between DPI and PPI?

DPI (Dots Per Inch) technically refers to printer resolution, while PPI (Pixels Per Inch) is the correct term for screens. However, they're often used interchangeably.

Why does my computer report 96 DPI when my screen is higher?

96 DPI is a logical reference value used by operating systems, not your actual physical pixel density. See our 72 vs 96 DPI guide for the full story.

Is higher DPI always better?

Not necessarily. Beyond certain thresholds (like Retina displays), additional pixel density provides diminishing returns while increasing GPU requirements and cost.

How do I calculate my screen's actual DPI?
